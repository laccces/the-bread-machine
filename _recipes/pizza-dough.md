# Plain Flour Pizza Dough (Easy Stretch, Steel-Optimised)

Designed for:
- Plain white flour
- Pizza steel
- 250°C fan oven
- Easy handling and good browning

Makes **2 × 280 g dough balls**

---

## Ingredients

- Plain white flour: **330 g**
- Water: **190 g**
- Instant yeast: **½ tsp**
- Fine salt: **8 g**
- Sugar: **6 g**
- Olive oil: **2½ tsp**

Hydration: ~58%

---

## Method

1. **Yeast**
   - Mix water and yeast.
   - Rest 2–3 minutes.

2. **Mix**
   - Add liquid to flour.
   - Mix until just combined.

3. **Oil**
   - Add olive oil and mix in.

4. **Season**
   - Add salt and sugar.
   - Mix to a shaggy dough.

5. **Knead**
   - Knead lightly for **3–4 minutes**.
   - Stop once smooth. Do not overwork.

6. **Rest**
   - Cover and rest **15 minutes**.

7. **Ball**
   - Divide into two **280 g** balls.
   - Shape gently with minimal tension.

8. **Cold ferment**
   - Lightly oil, cover, refrigerate **24–48 hours**.
   - 48 hours ideal. Do not exceed 72.

---

## Baking Day

### Dough prep
- Remove from fridge **90–120 minutes** before baking.
- Dough should be soft, cool, and relaxed.

---

## Oven Setup

- Pizza steel on **top third rack**
- Fan oven at **250°C**
- Preheat **45–60 minutes**

---

## Shaping

1. Flour dough and bench.
2. Press from centre outwards, leave rim untouched.
3. Flip and repeat.
4. Stretch gently over knuckles.

Target **10–11 inches**.  
If it resists, rest 5 minutes and continue.

---

## Topping

- Light sauce
- **80–90 g** mozzarella
- Avoid over-topping

---

## Bake

1. Launch onto steel.
2. Bake **6–7 minutes**.
3. Finish under grill **30–60 seconds** if needed for colour.

---

## Result

- Crisp underside
- Well-coloured rim
- Soft, non-chewy crumb
- Easy stretch, minimal snap-back
